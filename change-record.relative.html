Change a name or update a record

Use this page to find official information about changing a name, getting a certificate or correcting a record.

This service gives general information. It does not give legal advice.

Change your name

You may be able to apply to change your name.

You may need:

  • an application for change of name
  • a sworn affidavit
  • your birth certificate
  • your ID
  • parental consent in some cases

The official guidance says a change of name costs BDS $200 and usually takes 6 to 8 weeks.

Get a birth, death or marriage certificate

You can apply for official copies of birth, death and marriage certificates.

Applications are made through the Registration Department at the Supreme Court Complex, Whitepark Road, St. Michael.

The official guidance lists these fees:

  • birth certificate: BDS $5
  • birth certificate for persons 60 and over: BDS $1
  • death certificate: BDS $5
  • cause of death certificate: BDS $10
  • marriage certificate for nationals: BDS $10
  • marriage certificate for non-nationals: BDS $20
  • Apostille, if needed for overseas use: BDS $50

Fees may change. Check the official page before applying.

Correct an error on a birth certificate

If there is an error on a birth certificate, you may need to contact the Senior Legal Assistant in the Records Section.

A correction may be requested by:

  • the mother of the child
  • the father of the child
  • a relative who is an adult and at least 5 years older than the child
  • another person who knows about the error

You may need:

  • the certificate with the error
  • ID for the person asking for the correction
  • a sworn affidavit about the correction

Add a father’s name to a birth record

The father of a child who is not married to the mother may be able to have his name added to the child’s birth record.

You may need:

  • a signed declaration by both parents
  • the child’s birth certificate
  • ID for both parents
  • a sworn declaration by the child, if the child is over 18

The official guidance says the process can be completed the same day if all required documents are produced.

The only cost listed is the cost of a new birth certificate.
