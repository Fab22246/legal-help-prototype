Prepare information before speaking to a lawyer

Use this page to organise basic information before speaking to a lawyer.

You do not need to have everything before you speak to a lawyer.

Do not include private details unless you are comfortable doing so.

This service does not give legal advice.

What to write down

Before speaking to a lawyer, write down:

  • what happened
  • when it happened
  • who is involved
  • what you have already done
  • what you are worried about
  • what you want help with

Documents to gather

Bring any documents you already have.

This may include:

  • a will
  • a death certificate
  • a birth or marriage certificate
  • a Barbados ID
  • a letter or notice
  • a court document
  • a bank, credit union or insurance document
  • a land or property document
  • receipts, bills or invoices

You can still speak to a lawyer if you do not have all the documents.

Questions to ask

You may want to ask:

  • What legal step may apply?
  • What documents do I need?
  • Who should sign the document?
  • Does the document need to be witnessed, certified or sworn?
  • Where should the document be submitted or filed?
  • Are there any deadlines?
  • What will it cost?
  • What should I do next?

If the issue is urgent or disputed

Tell the lawyer if:

  • there is already a court matter
  • someone may challenge a will
  • family members disagree
  • someone is pressuring you
  • land, a business or overseas property is involved
  • a deadline is coming soon

Prototype note

This prototype gives general information.

It does not create legal documents, file applications, take payments, book appointments or save your information yet.
