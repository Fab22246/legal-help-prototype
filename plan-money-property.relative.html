Plan what happens to my money and property

Use this page to think about what should happen to your money, property and belongings.

You can use it to:

  • make a list of what you own
  • prepare information for a simple will
  • understand the difference between a will and giving property while you are alive
  • think about where important documents are kept
  • prepare before speaking to a lawyer

This service gives general information. It does not give legal advice.

Planning options

Prepare a simple will

A will says what should happen to your money, property and belongings after you die.

It can also name the person who should manage your estate. This person is called an executor.

Prepare a simple will

Give property while you are alive

Giving property to someone while you are alive is different from leaving property in a will.

A deed of gift may transfer property while you are alive.

This can affect what you own, where you live, your future needs and your estate.

Prepare before speaking to a lawyer

You may want to speak to a lawyer before making decisions about land, a house, business interests, overseas property or family disagreements.

You can prepare your questions, documents and information first.

Prepare information before speaking to a lawyer

Make a list of what you own

Before preparing a will or speaking to a lawyer, it can help to make a list of:

  • bank or credit union accounts
  • insurance policies
  • land or a house
  • vehicles
  • shares or investments
  • valuable personal items
  • debts, loans, mortgages or charges
  • business interests
  • property outside Barbados

This prototype does not create an asset list yet.

Choose who should manage your estate

The person who manages your estate after you die is called an executor.

They may need to:

  • collect your money and property
  • pay debts
  • deal with banks, insurers or government offices
  • carry out what the will says

Choose someone you trust.

You may want to speak to them before naming them.

Think about who may need to be considered

Before preparing a will, think about whether you have:

  • a spouse or partner you live with as if married
  • a child under 18
  • a child with a disability who depends on you
  • someone else who depends on you financially

If you are not sure, or if you want to leave someone out, you should speak to a lawyer before preparing a will.

Store important documents safely

Keep important documents somewhere safe.

This may include:

  • your signed will
  • ID documents
  • land or house documents
  • insurance documents
  • bank or credit union information
  • vehicle documents
  • loan, mortgage or debt information

Tell someone you trust where to find them.

Safekeeping of wills

The Records Branch is part of the Barbados court system.

It provides a free safekeeping service for original signed wills.

This means the original will can be stored safely after it has been signed and witnessed.

This is different from filing a court application.

The official guidance says the original will must be placed in a sealed envelope.

The envelope should include:

  • the date of the will
  • the name and address of the person making the will
  • the names and addresses of the executors

When to speak to a lawyer

You should speak to a lawyer before taking action if:

  • someone may challenge a will
  • someone is pressuring you to sign or give away property
  • people disagree about money, property or an estate
  • land or a house is owned with someone else
  • a business or part of a business is involved
  • property outside Barbados is involved
  • there is already a court matter
  • you are not sure what a document does
