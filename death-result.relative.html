You may need probate

Why you are seeing this

You said the person left a will and you are named as executor.

Probate may be needed when the executor needs authority to deal with the person’s estate.

What this means

Probate is an official document from the Supreme Court.

It gives the executor authority to deal with the person’s estate.

Banks, credit unions, insurance companies or other organisations may ask for probate before they release money or property.

What you may need

You may need:

  • the original will
  • the death certificate
  • details of the person’s money, property and debts
  • names and contact details for banks, credit unions, insurers or other organisations
  • legal help to prepare and file the application

What to do next

Check the will and gather the documents you have.

You may need to speak to a lawyer before applying for probate.

You may need letters of administration

Why you are seeing this

You said the person did not leave a will and the estate may be worth more than BDS $15,000.

Letters of administration may be needed when someone needs authority to deal with the estate of a person who died without a will.

What this means

Letters of administration are official documents from the Supreme Court.

They give an administrator authority to deal with the estate.

Banks, credit unions, insurance companies or other organisations may ask for letters of administration before they release money or property.

What you may need

You may need:

  • the death certificate
  • documents that show your relationship to the person who died
  • details of the person’s money, property and debts
  • names and contact details for banks, credit unions, insurers or other organisations
  • legal help to prepare and file the application

What to do next

Gather the documents you have.

You should speak to a lawyer if you are not sure who should apply.

The Public Trustee may be able to help

Why you are seeing this

You said the estate may be worth BDS $15,000 or less, or that you want to know if the Public Trustee can help.

The Public Trustee may be able to help with a small estate.

What this means

This may apply if the person’s estate is worth BDS $15,000 or less.

The person applying usually needs to show their relationship to the person who died.

What you may need

You may need:

  • the death certificate
  • the person’s Barbados ID card
  • the person’s National Insurance number
  • funeral bill or invoice
  • funeral payment receipts
  • documents that show your relationship to the person who died
  • bank, credit union, insurance, share or vehicle documents, if relevant

What to do next

Gather the documents you have.

A Public Trustee application must include the required documents before it can be processed.

The application form must be signed and witnessed by a Justice of the Peace or attorney-at-law.

Copies of supporting documents may need to be certified.

You may need estate clearance

Why you are seeing this

You said you want to get estate clearance.

Estate clearance may be needed before estate assets are distributed.

What this means

This usually happens after probate or letters of administration has been granted.

What you may need

You may need:

  • a completed and signed estate clearance application
  • the death certificate
  • letters testamentary or letters of administration
  • a certified copy of the will, if there is one
  • a list of assets
  • the map reference number for real property, if land or a house is involved

What to do next

Gather the documents you have.

Estate clearance applications can be sent to the Barbados Revenue Authority.
