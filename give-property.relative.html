Give property while you are alive

Giving property to someone while you are alive is different from leaving property in a will.

A will says what should happen after you die.

A deed of gift may transfer property while you are alive.

This service gives general information. It does not give legal advice.

Before you give property

Before giving property to someone, you may need to understand:

  • whether you own the property by yourself
  • whether there is a mortgage, charge, loan or debt attached to it
  • whether anyone else has a legal interest in it
  • whether land tax, stamp duty or other duties may apply
  • whether the transfer needs to be registered
  • whether giving the property affects where you live, your money, your care or your estate

Land or a house

You should speak to a lawyer if you want to give land or a house to someone while you are alive.

A transfer of land or a house may need:

  • title or ownership documents
  • land tax clearance
  • duty assessment or stamping
  • registration
  • legal advice before signing

Do not sign a deed of gift unless you understand what it does.

Money or personal items

Giving money or personal items may be simpler than giving land or a house.

You should still think about:

  • whether the item belongs only to you
  • whether anyone may disagree
  • whether you need proof of the gift
  • whether the gift affects your future needs or your estate

If someone is pressuring you

Do not use this service as your only source of help if someone is pressuring you to give away property.

You should speak to a lawyer or another trusted professional before signing anything.

What to do next

Prepare information before speaking to a lawyer if you are thinking about giving property to someone.

This can help you organise:

  • what you want to give
  • who you want to give it to
  • proof that you own it
  • any debts, loans or charges attached to it
  • any documents you already have
